Introduction

These are my notes from Miguel Hernán’s book on Causal Inference

Sources of Random Variability

  1. Sampling Variability - sample value not equal to the population value we are trying to estimate.
  2. Nondeterministic counterfactuals - an individual’s counterfactual outcomes might not be fixed in advance.

Definitions

  • \(Y^{a = 1}, Y^{a = 0}\) Potential outcomes, counterfactual outcomes. What would have occured, had the individual been treated with \(A\) and also if not treated. May not actually occur.
    • If an individual, i, is treated, then \(A = 1\) and \(Y_i^{a = 1} = 1 = Y_i\)
  • Causal effect for an individual: the treatment has a causal effect on the ind outcome Y if $ = Y^{a = 0}$
  • Consistency - An individual with observed outcome A equal to \(a\) has observed outcome Y equal to his counterfactual outcome \(Y^a\).
