Assume that a dice is roll until 10 even numbers have been rolled. Denote \(X_i\) as the number of rolls that land on side \(i\).

Evens

Since we know that we have 10 even rolls then each \(X_2, X_4, X_6\) are binomial with \(n = 10\) and \(p = 1/3\). Thus, \[ E[X_2] = E[X_4] = E[X_6] = \frac{10}{3} \]

Odds

Let \(N\) be the total number of dice rolls until 10 even numbers are rolled. This is a negative binomial distribution with parameters \(r = 10\) and \(p = 1/2\). \[ P(N = n) = {n - 1 \choose 10} (1/2)^{n} \quad \text{for }n = 10, 11, \ldots \] with \(E[N] = 20\).

Given a fixed \(N = n\), then \(X_1 | N = n\) is binomial with size \(n - 10\) and probability \(p = 1/3\). \[ E[X_1 | N = n] = \frac{n - 10}{3} \] Then we can compute \(E[X_1]\) as follows \[ \begin{aligned} E[X_1] &= E[E[X_1 | N = n]]\\ &= \sum_{n = 10}^\infty E[X_1 | N = n] P(N = n)\\ &= \sum_{n = 10}^\infty \frac{n - 10}{3} P(N = n)\\ &= \frac{1}{3} \sum_{n = 10}^\infty n P(N = n) - \frac{10}{3} \underbrace{\sum_{n = 10}^\infty P(N = n)}_{1}\\ &= \frac{1}{3} E[N] - \frac{10}{3}\\ &= \frac{20 - 10}{3}\\ &= \frac{10}{3} \end{aligned} \]
